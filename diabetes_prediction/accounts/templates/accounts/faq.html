Frequently Asked Questions

The Diabetes Prediction System is an advanced tool that uses machine learning algorithms to assess your risk of developing diabetes based on various health factors, lifestyle choices, and medical history. It provides personalized recommendations to help you manage and reduce your risk.

Our prediction model has been trained on a large dataset and has an accuracy of approximately 85-90%. However, it's important to note that this is not a medical diagnosis. The prediction is based on statistical analysis and should be used as a screening tool only. Always consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

For most people, checking your diabetes risk once every 6-12 months is sufficient. However, if you've made significant lifestyle changes or have been diagnosed with prediabetes, you might want to check more frequently (every 3-6 months) to monitor your progress. Always follow the advice of your healthcare provider regarding the frequency of health assessments.

If your prediction indicates a high risk of diabetes, we recommend:

  1. Consult with a healthcare professional as soon as possible
  2. Review the personalized recommendations provided by our system
  3. Consider lifestyle modifications such as improved diet and increased physical activity
  4. Monitor your blood glucose levels regularly if advised by your doctor
  5. Follow up with regular health check-ups

Remember, early intervention can significantly reduce your risk of developing diabetes.
